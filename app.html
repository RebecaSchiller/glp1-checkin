Curated tools, questions, and reading to help you network with confidence. Plain. Practical. No filler.

Questions to Ask
Informational Interview
  • →How did you get into this field, and what does your career path look like in hindsight?
  • →What does a typical day or week look like for you?
  • →What do you wish you had known before entering this industry?
  • →What skills or experiences do you think matter most for someone looking to break in?
  • →Is there anyone else you think I should talk to?
Coffee Chat
  • →What's keeping you busy these days?
  • →What's the most interesting thing you're working on right now?
  • →How has your industry changed in the last few years?
  • →What do you look for when you're hiring or recommending someone?
  • →Is there anything I can help you with or anyone I can connect you to?
Follow-Up Call
  • →I took your advice on [X] — here's what happened. Does that change anything for you?
  • →Since we last spoke, I've been focused on [X]. Am I headed in the right direction?
  • →Are there any new developments on your end I should know about?
  • →Is there a better time to check back in, or is now a good window?
  • →Who else do you think I should be talking to at this stage?
Networking Events
  • →What brought you to this event?
  • →What kind of work are you focused on right now?
  • →What's been the most useful thing you've gotten from events like this?
  • →Would you be open to staying in touch? I'd love to connect on LinkedIn.
20 Networking Tips
TIP 1
Don't burn bridges.
The professional world is smaller than it looks. The person you dismiss today may be hiring tomorrow.
TIP 2
Start before you need to.
The best time to build your network is when you don't need anything. The second best time is now.
TIP 3
Give before you ask.
Share an article, make an introduction, offer a referral. Generosity opens more doors than any pitch.
TIP 4
Be specific in your ask.
"Can I pick your brain?" is not a request. "Can I ask you two questions about breaking into nonprofit communications?" is.
TIP 5
Follow up within 24 hours.
A quick note after a meeting cements the connection. Wait too long and the moment passes.
TIP 6
Keep it short.
People are busy. A three-sentence email gets more responses than a three-paragraph one.
TIP 7
Research before you reach out.
Know what they do, what they've worked on, and why you're reaching out to them specifically.
TIP 8
Connect on LinkedIn immediately.
Don't let a good conversation disappear into a business card pile. Connect while the interaction is fresh.
TIP 9
Have a clear one-liner.
Know how to describe what you do and what you're looking for in one confident sentence.
TIP 10
Ask for introductions, not jobs.
"Do you know anyone I should talk to?" is far easier to say yes to than "Do you know of any openings?"
TIP 11
Send value, not just updates.
When you check back in, bring something — a relevant article, a congrats on their news, a shared contact.
TIP 12
Warm up cold contacts.
Comment on their posts, share their content, or reference their work before you reach out directly.
TIP 13
Don't neglect weak ties.
Acquaintances — not close friends — are often where the best opportunities come from. Stay in touch with the periphery.
TIP 14
Personalize every message.
Never send the same message twice. Even one specific detail shows you paid attention.
TIP 15
Accept help gracefully.
When someone offers to help, say yes. Letting people contribute builds the relationship.
TIP 16
Be honest about where you are.
You don't have to have it all figured out. People respond to honesty and clarity about what you're working toward.
TIP 17
Follow through on what you say.
If you say you'll send something, send it. Reliability is memorable.
TIP 18
Stay in touch even when nothing's happening.
A quick "thinking of you" or "saw this and thought of you" keeps the relationship alive without an agenda.
TIP 19
Treat every conversation as practice.
Not every contact leads somewhere. That's fine. Each one makes the next one easier.
TIP 20
Say thank you. Always.
A genuine thank you — specific, timely, and brief — is one of the most underused tools in networking.

Step 1 of 3

Let's get you set up.

Network Momentum keeps your networking contacts organized so you always know who to reach out to next. Here's what you'll need:

  • A Google account to store your contacts
  • About 5 minutes to connect everything
  • An Anthropic API key if you want AI suggestions (optional)
Step 2 of 3

Connect your Google Sheet.

Your contacts live in a Google Sheet you own. Nothing is stored on our servers.

  • Go to sheets.google.com and create a blank spreadsheet
  • Rename the first tab exactly: Contacts
  • Click Extensions → Apps Script, paste the setup script, and deploy as a Web App
  • Copy the Web App URL and paste it into Settings below

Need help getting set up? Visit networkmomentum.app/setup

Step 3 of 3

Set your weekly goal.

How many new contacts do you want to reach out to each week? A realistic goal keeps you consistent without burning out.

  • 5 contacts/week is a solid starting point
  • Your progress bar updates in real time
  • You can change this any time in Settings

You're all set. Add your first contact and start building momentum.
